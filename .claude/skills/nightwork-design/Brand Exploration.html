Nightwork — eight brand directions

Same dashboard, same copy, same components. Only palette + type + chrome change. Tell me which ones to keep exploring and I'll push those further.

00 · Current (baseline)
Teal chrome · cream page · amber accent. The version you don't love. Here for comparison.
01 · Monochrome rigor
Pure white + black. No accent. Linear / Vercel energy. Status colors do all the talking. Feels the most serious and timeless; also the most austere.
02 · Ink & iron
Warm stone page · near-black chrome · deep blue accent. Technical, precise. Blue only lives in links, numbers, primary CTAs.
03 · Blueprint
Cool paper · deep navy chrome · cyan accent. Leans construction-blueprint. Feels trustworthy + institutional.
04 · Forest ledger
Keep the cream · deep forest green chrome · muted brass accent. Money + build together. Feels like a fountain-pen ledger.
05 · Oxblood & paper
Warm off-white · near-black brown chrome · oxblood accent · serif display. Old-money ledger. Most distinctive here; farthest from "SaaS".
06 · Bone & black
Warm bone page · white chrome · all-black text & CTAs. Ramp-style. Quiet and expensive; no colored accents at all.
07 · Brass & slate
Cool neutral page · slate-blue chrome · brass accent. Swaps the orange for a warmer, muted gold. Closer to current chrome, new warmth.
08 · Terra & oak
Warm sand · dark oak chrome · terracotta accent. Earthy, construction-honest. Deeper, richer take on an orange — still an "orange" family, but oxidized, not fluorescent.
